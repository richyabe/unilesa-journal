About UJSMS

The UNILESA Journal of Social and Management Sciences (UJSMS) is a peer-reviewed, open-access academic journal published by the University of Lagos (UNILESA). Established in 2026, UJSMS provides a premier platform for scholars, researchers, and practitioners to disseminate original research in the broad fields of social and management sciences.

UJSMS is committed to rigorous academic standards, employing a double-blind peer review process to ensure the quality and integrity of every publication. The journal serves as a bridge between academic scholarship and real-world policy, management practice, and social development.

Published quarterly, UJSMS serves researchers from Nigeria, across Africa, and the global academic community. Its interdisciplinary character encourages cross-disciplinary dialogue among economists, sociologists, public administrators, business scholars, and development practitioners.

Our Purpose

Mission & Vision

🎯

Our Mission

To advance the frontiers of knowledge in social and management sciences by providing a high-quality, open-access platform for the publication of original, rigorous, and policy-relevant scholarly research. UJSMS is committed to fostering interdisciplinary inquiry that bridges theory and practice, serving scholars and institutions across Nigeria, Africa, and the global academic community.

🔭

Our Vision

To be Africa's leading peer-reviewed journal in social and management sciences — recognized globally for academic excellence, editorial integrity, and transformative scholarship that drives sustainable development, informed policy, and innovative management practice on the continent and beyond.

Editorial Policy

🔍 Peer Review Process

All submitted manuscripts undergo a rigorous double-blind peer review by at least two independent experts in the relevant field. The identities of both authors and reviewers are kept confidential throughout the process. Editorial decisions are based solely on scholarly merit, originality, and contribution to the field.

⚖️ Editorial Independence

The editorial board operates independently of commercial interests. Editorial decisions are never influenced by advertisers, sponsors, or institutional pressures. The Editor-in-Chief has final authority on all publication decisions.

⏱️ Review Timeline

  • Initial screening1–2 weeks
  • Peer review4–8 weeks
  • Revision period2–4 weeks
  • Final decision to publication2–4 weeks

🌐 Open Access Policy

UJSMS is a fully open-access journal. All published articles are freely available online immediately upon publication under a Creative Commons Attribution (CC-BY 4.0) license. Authors retain copyright of their work.

Integrity

Publication Ethics

UJSMS adheres to the highest standards of publication ethics consistent with COPE (Committee on Publication Ethics) guidelines.

Authors' Responsibilities

  • ✓ Submit only original, unpublished work
  • ✓ Accurate acknowledgement of all sources
  • ✓ Declare conflicts of interest
  • ✓ List all contributing authors fairly
  • ✓ Notify errors promptly for correction
  • ✓ Obtain ethical clearance where required

Reviewers' Responsibilities

  • ✓ Provide objective, constructive feedback
  • ✓ Maintain confidentiality of manuscripts
  • ✓ Disclose potential conflicts of interest
  • ✓ Complete reviews within agreed timeframe
  • ✓ Alert editors to ethical concerns
  • ✓ Not use manuscript content for personal gain

Editors' Responsibilities

  • ✓ Fair and unbiased evaluation of all submissions
  • ✓ Maintain confidentiality of authors and reviewers
  • ✓ Disclose and manage conflicts of interest
  • ✓ Investigate misconduct allegations thoroughly
  • ✓ Issue corrections and retractions when necessary
  • ✓ Preserve integrity of the academic record

⚠️ Misconduct & Plagiarism Policy

UJSMS uses plagiarism detection software (iThenticate / Turnitin) to screen all submissions. A similarity index exceeding 20% (excluding references) will result in desk rejection. Fabrication of data, duplicate submission, and authorship disputes are treated with zero tolerance. Confirmed cases of misconduct will result in article retraction and notification of the author's institution.
